‚ùì Essential Questions Within the Big Idea

Essential questions are broad, open-ended, and probe the core of the problem. Considering these will lead you to specific, solvable challenges.

  • Systemic & Economic Questions: How can Sri Lanka transition from a linear "dispose-of" waste management model to a circular "resource-recovery" model? What circular business models (e.g., product-as-a-service, sharing platforms) could be viable in the Sri Lankan context ? How can we design out waste and pollution from the very beginning ?
  • Technical & Technological Questions: How can we efficiently sort and process different waste streams (plastic, organic, C&D waste) in Sri Lanka? What affordable and robust technologies can be developed for recycling and waste-to-energy conversion ? How can automation and IoT be used to create smarter, more efficient waste collection and management systems?
  • Social & Infrastructural Questions: How can we improve household and community participation in waste separation and recycling programs ? How can the informal sector (e.g., waste pickers) be integrated and valued in a formal circular economy ? What are the most significant barriers to implementing circular economy strategies in specific sectors like construction ?

  • Selected Challenge: Designing a small-scale, decentralized anaerobic digester for organic waste.

  • Why Selected?

    1. High Impact: Organic waste constitutes a large portion of Sri Lanka's municipal solid waste. Tackling this reduces landfill burden and creates valuable energy and fertilizer .
    2. Perfect Skill Fit: This challenge requires mechanical engineering skills (reactor vessel design, thermodynamics, fluid dynamics, structural integrity) and mechatronics skills (sensors for temperature/pH monitoring, automated gas collection and safety systems, control systems for optimizing the digestion process).
    3. Scalability and Practicality: A small-scale solution is ideal for a university project and has real-world application for hotels, farms, or small communities, making the project highly relevant.
    4. Circularity in Action: It perfectly embodies the circular economy principle of turning waste into resources, regenerating living systems, and keeping materials in use .
