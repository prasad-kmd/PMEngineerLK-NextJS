Project Diary: The Journey

Week 1: Identifying the Big Idea

Our initial brainstorming sessions revolved around finding a topic that was both challenging and meaningful. The supervisor's suggestion to explore outside our direct field was considered, but the team felt a strong pull towards applying our engineering skills to a pressing national problem. The consensus was that gaining career-relevant experience was a priority.

We settled on "Waste Management & a Circular Economy". It felt right. It's a visible, tangible problem in Sri Lanka, and we could see immediate potential for mechanical and mechatronics solutions.

Week 2: Selecting the Core Challenge

4. Why We Selected Our Challenge

After listing several potential challenges in our Ideas document, we had to make a crucial decision. We chose: Challenge #1: Inefficient and Hazardous Manual Waste Sorting.

This choice was deliberate for the following reasons:

  • Direct Application of Skills: It is a classic mechatronics problem. It requires a system with sensors (to identify materials), actuators and robotic arms (to sort), and a mechanical conveyor system (to move the waste). This aligns perfectly with our team's skillset.
  • High Impact Potential: Automating this process would not only dramatically increase the speed and purity of recyclables but also remove human workers from a dangerous and unhygienic environment. This has both economic and ethical benefits.
  • Feasibility for a Prototype: While a full-scale industrial plant is beyond our scope, designing and building a small-scale, functional prototype is an achievable and impressive goal for an undergraduate project. We can demonstrate the proof-of-concept effectively.
  • Innovation Space: There is room for innovation in sensor fusion (e.g., combining computer vision with inductive sensors for metals), sorting algorithms, and cost-effective robotic gripper design.

Our project mission is now clear: To design and build a prototype of an automated waste sorting system to improve the efficiency and safety of recycling in Sri Lanka.
