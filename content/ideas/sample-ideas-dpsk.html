Big Idea 1: Precision Agriculture & Food Security

Sri Lanka's economy is heavily reliant on agriculture, but it faces challenges like water scarcity, high post-harvest losses, and unpredictable weather.

Why it's good for your group: This applies core Mechanical (structures, fluids, thermodynamics) and Mechatronics (sensors, control systems, automation) principles to a non-engineering field.

Potential Project Challenges/Solutions:

  • Challenge: Optimizing water usage in paddy or vegetable cultivation.
    • Solution: Design a low-cost, solar-powered, IoT-based smart irrigation system. (Mechatronics: moisture sensors, microcontroller, valve control. Mechanical: pump selection, fluid dynamics, solar panel mounting).
  • Challenge: Reducing post-harvest losses of fruits (like bananas, mangoes) and vegetables during storage and transport.
    • Solution: Develop a modular, passive-cooling storage unit for small-scale farmers. (Mechanical: thermodynamics, heat transfer, material selection for insulation. Mechatronics: simple temperature/humidity monitoring).
  • Challenge: Inefficient and labor-intensive process of tea plucking.
    • Solution: Prototype a semi-autonomous, robotic tea-plucking aid that can identify "two leaves and a bud." (Mechatronics: computer vision, robotic arm control. Mechanical: end-effector design, lightweight chassis).

Big Idea 2: Sustainable Fisheries & Marine Conservation

The fishing industry is vital for nutrition and livelihood, but it's threatened by overfishing, fuel costs, and bycatch.

Why it's good for your group: This involves marine mechanics, robotics, and sensor systems—a perfect blend for your skillset.

Potential Project Challenges/Solutions:

  • Challenge: High fuel consumption of traditional outrigger canoes (“oruwa”).
    • Solution: Re-design the hull or propulsion system for better efficiency, or design a retrofit hybrid (solar-electric) auxiliary system. (Mechanical: fluid dynamics, propulsion, structural integrity. Mechatronics: motor control, battery management).
  • Challenge: Destructive bottom trawling and high bycatch (catching non-target species).
    • Solution: Design a "smart net" or a device that uses acoustic or visual cues to selectively allow certain species to escape. (Mechatronics: underwater sensors, simple actuation. Mechanical: net material dynamics, hydrodynamic shape).
  • Challenge: Monitoring of marine protected areas and illegal fishing.
    • Solution: Develop a low-cost, autonomous surface vehicle (ASV) or underwater glider for coastal monitoring. (Mechatronics: navigation, control systems, communication. Mechanical: watertight hull design, buoyancy control).

Big Idea 3: Waste Management & Circular Economy

Sri Lanka has a severe and growing solid waste problem, particularly with plastics and organic waste.

Why it's good for your group: This field requires innovative machine design, process automation, and systems thinking.

Potential Project Challenges/Solutions:

  • Challenge: Inefficient and low-rate recycling of plastic waste.
    • Solution: Design and build a small-scale, low-energy plastic shredder and extruder to create filament for 3D printing or new products. (Mechanical: machine design, thermal systems, mechanics of materials. Mechatronics: motor speed control, temperature regulation).
  • Challenge: Managing organic waste (kitchen, market) which leads to methane emissions in landfills.
    • Solution: Create an optimized, small-scale anaerobic digester for a community or hotel, with a mechanism to capture and utilize biogas. (Mechanical: reactor vessel design, gas storage, heat exchange. Mechatronics: pH/temperature sensors, gas flow monitoring).
  • Challenge: Cleaning canals and water bodies clogged with plastic waste.
    • Solution: Design a riverine or canal trash collection robot that is low-cost and easy to maintain. (Mechanical: conveyor mechanism, floatation design. Mechatronics: remote control or autonomous navigation to avoid obstacles).

Big Idea 4: Accessible Healthcare & Medical Devices

There is a need for affordable, locally-made medical devices, especially for rural areas and rehabilitation.

Why it's good for your group: Biomedical engineering is a premier field that heavily relies on mechatronics and mechanical design principles.

Potential Project Challenges/Solutions:

  • Challenge: Lack of affordable physical therapy and rehabilitation devices.
    • Solution: Design a low-cost, mechanical/motorized device for hand or limb rehabilitation that can be used at home. (Mechatronics: precise motor control, safety interlocks. Mechanical: ergonomics, linkage design, force feedback).
  • Challenge: Maintaining the cold chain for vaccine transport to remote clinics.
    • Solution: Develop a robust, portable vaccine carrier with a phase-change material and a monitoring system to ensure temperature integrity. (Mechanical: advanced insulation, thermal mass design. Mechatronics: GPS & temperature logger with alerts).
  • Challenge: Difficulty in diagnosing respiratory issues like asthma in low-resource settings.
    • Solution: Create a simple, digital peak flow meter or spirometer with a mobile app interface for data tracking. (Mechanical: airflow sensing mechanism. Mechatronics: pressure sensor, microcontroller, Bluetooth communication).
